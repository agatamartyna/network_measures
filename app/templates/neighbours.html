Generate Phonological Neighbours and Neighbourhood Density

Neighbourhood density is a measure indicating the number of a word's phonological neighbours. A word's phonological neighbour is a sequence differing from it by one phoneme by virtue of replacement (subsititution), deletion or insertion of one phoneme. Thus, among the phonological neighbours of the Polish word 'kot' are 'kit' (replacing the phoneme /o/ with /i/), 'kotu' (inserting the phoneme /u/), or 'od' (deleting the initial phoneme /k/). This device only generates neighbours of Polish words.
